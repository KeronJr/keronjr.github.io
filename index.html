About

I own both uptime and security.

Systems that can't afford to go down — in the regulated, low-connectivity, on-premise conditions African fintech, government, and NGO programs actually run in.

I started in software engineering — designing, prototyping, and building things end to end — and over time my focus moved toward what I care about most: cybersecurity and systems infrastructure. I still write code; it makes me sharper on both sides. But my work now centers on keeping systems secure, resilient, and online.

At Baron Sports Gaming I ran the Linux infrastructure behind real-time tax-tracking systems for national revenue authorities across three countries — Docker, Redis, monitoring, incident response, and the runbooks that let other engineers repeat my deployments without me in the room. Before that I deployed enterprise services — NFS, LDAP, Active Directory, DNS/DHCP — inside MTN Rwanda, one of Africa's largest telecoms.

Today I work security from the inside: vulnerability assessment, web-application penetration testing, and memory forensics at Bank of Kigali's technology arm. I'm comfortable taking a system from reconnaissance all the way through to a hardened, monitored, documented production service.
